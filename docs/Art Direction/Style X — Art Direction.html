The Brief

What Style X is, what it commits to, and why it exists as a system rather than a mood board.

Mission

Style X is the visual system for every book Bookit produces. It is not a mood board. It is a structured agreement between three distinct visual languages — each given jurisdiction over a specific layer of every document.

Most educational documents fail visually for one of two reasons: they play it too safe and become forgettable, or they lean too hard into one expressive style and become illegible. Style X solves this by assigning each aesthetic its own lane. Three styles. Three jurisdictions. One document.

The Three Commitments
  • I

    Content pages are always off-white.

    Background #f7f4ef. No dark backgrounds on reading surfaces. Print economy, screen legibility, and accessibility are non-negotiable constraints — not preferences.

  • II

    Color lives in components, not grounds.

    Saturated color appears in callout boxes, chapter openers, stat blocks, and diagrams. Never as a full-bleed page background in content areas. The page is quiet. The components carry the energy.

  • III

    Typography does the heavy lifting at display scale.

    Section numbers, chapter titles, and pull quotes use Space Grotesk 800 at extreme scale. Decorative illustration is not part of Style X — bold type replaces it.

The Three Sources

Where Style X comes from — and the specific DNA each source contributes to the system.

Source 01

Dark Atmospheric

Bioluminescent glows on near-black grounds. Flat silhouetted characters. Selective luminosity as the primary design tool. Light is earned, not assumed.

Fusion Rules
  • R01

    Dark backgrounds appear at chapter openers and summary boxes only.

    Source 01's dark atmospheric quality is reserved for the start and end of each chapter. Content pages between them are always off-white. Dark backgrounds must be earned by structural position — not used for visual variety.

    Source 01
  • R02

    All instructional components use flat solid color — no gradients.

    Callout boxes, stat blocks, and diagram frames use the vivid flat palette with solid color borders or fills. Gradients are prohibited in all components, all contexts.

    Source 02
  • R03

    Section numbers minimum 4rem. Pull quotes minimum 1.5rem.

    Source 03's contribution is the willingness to use scale. Small decorative numbers are not Style X. If a number or quote is large enough to read as a visual element on the page, it's doing its job.

    Source 03
  • R04

    Each book uses one primary accent color. Do not mix within a book.

    Consistency of accent color is how the reader builds a mental model of the book's visual language. Mixing accent colors breaks that coherence. One book, one accent.

    System rule
  • R05

    Cyan (#00BCD4) is reserved for dark backgrounds only.

    Cyan is the glow color — the accent that appears on the dark chapter opener and summary box. It does not appear in any component on an off-white background. This maintains its impact as a signal of structural transition.

    Source 01 System rule

Components

Every building block of a Bookit book, rendered in Style X with usage notes.

C-01 — Chapter Opener
01
Chapter One
What Is a Ulysses Pact?
A decision made during clarity designed to bind your future self when temptation arrives.
Source 01 DNA. Dark background (#0a0d18). Large faded chapter number (right-aligned, 8rem, 5% white opacity). Cyan eyebrow label. White title in Space Grotesk 800. Off-white content begins immediately below.
C-02 — Callout Boxes (5 types)
Key Concept

A Ulysses pact is a freely made decision designed to bind yourself in the future, preventing poor choices when you anticipate being in a compromised state of judgment.

Note

In medicine, the term appears in advance directives (living wills), though there is ongoing debate about whether decisions made in one health state should bind someone in a markedly different condition.

Important

Soft commitment devices may be less effective when baseline motivation is already strong. Hard commitments with clear penalties consistently outperform soft versions in controlled trials.

Evidence

Financial stakes increase follow-through rates 2–3× compared to pledge-only conditions (StickK platform data, 409-participant meta-analysis).

Critical

Enforcing a commitment contract when a patient has changed their mind raises serious ethical concerns — particularly for individuals who retain decision-making capacity during a crisis.

Source 02 DNA. 4px solid left border. White fill (never tinted). Label: 0.6rem, 700, tracked caps. Five types — color signals semantic meaning. No gradients. Margins: 1.25rem top and bottom.
C-03 — Pull Quote
"You don't eliminate the temptation — you remove the possibility of giving in to it."
— Nick Wignall · Ulysses Pact
Source 03 DNA. Space Grotesk 600, minimum 1.5rem, tight tracking. 2px solid ink border top and bottom — no background color, no box. Attribution in caption size below. One pull quote per major section maximum.
C-04 — Definition Box
Commitment Device

A pre-commitment mechanism that restricts future choices to prevent anticipated poor decisions. Most effective when designed during a moment of clarity before temptation or weakness arrives.

White card with 1px border. Term in Space Grotesk 700. 32px × 2px accent-color rule beneath the term. Body text in Inter, ink-2. Use on first occurrence of each technical term only — never repeat.
C-05 — Key Stat
82.8%
Success rate with financial stakes on the line
3×
More likely to hit goals vs. no stakes
2×
Improvement over willpower alone
Source 03 DNA (scale) + Source 02 DNA (color). Space Grotesk 800, minimum 2.5rem. Number color = book accent color. 1–3 columns separated by 1px border lines. White cell backgrounds. Never use more than 3 stats per block.
C-06 — Chapter Summary
Chapter Summary
  • A Ulysses pact is a pre-commitment made during clarity to bind future behavior under anticipated weakness
  • The strategy works by removing temptation, creating barriers, or adding meaningful friction to bad choices
  • Financial stakes raise success rates from ~50% to 82.8% — the cost of backing out must be real
  • Social accountability adds ~20% improvement beyond financial stakes alone
Source 01 DNA. Dark background (#0a0d18) — mirrors the chapter opener and bookends the chapter. Cyan eyebrow label. Em dash bullet in cyan. White/75% body text. Always the last element in a chapter before the next opener.

## STYLE X — BOOKIT VISUAL SYSTEM PROMPT
## Paste this block into any LLM context when generating Bookit book content.

# ── GROUND RULES ──────────────────────────────────
page_background:        #f7f4ef   (warm off-white — always, no exceptions)
content_card_surface:   #ffffff   (component boxes, definition cards)
dark_bg:                #0a0d18   (chapter openers + summary boxes ONLY)
PROHIBITED:             gradients on any component, any context
PROHIBITED:             dark backgrounds on reading/body pages
PROHIBITED:             multiple accent colors within one book
PROHIBITED:             cyan (#00BCD4) on off-white backgrounds

# ── TYPOGRAPHY ─────────────────────────────────────
display_font:           Space Grotesk, weight 800, tracking -0.055em
body_font:              Inter, weight 400, line-height 1.65
chapter_title:          Space Grotesk 800, clamp(2.5rem, 6vw, 4rem)
section_number:         minimum 4rem — larger is always correct
pull_quote:             Space Grotesk 600, minimum 1.5rem
body_color:             #4b5060 (ink-2) — never pure black on off-white
caption_color:          #9ca3af (ink-3)

# ── COLOR ROLES ────────────────────────────────────
accent_per_book:        ONE color only — assign at project creation
default_accent:         #3525CD (Bookit indigo)
callout_key_concept:    #3525CD border + label
callout_note:           #005CE6 border + label
callout_important:      #F59E0B border + label
callout_evidence:       #43A047 border + label
callout_critical:       #E03C31 border + label
dark_layer_accent:      #00BCD4 (cyan) — labels on dark bg only
stat_number_color:      book accent color

# ── CHAPTER STRUCTURE ──────────────────────────────
chapter_open:           dark bg + faded chapter num (right) + cyan label + white title
chapter_body:           off-white ground, white component surfaces
chapter_close:          summary box: dark bg + cyan label + 4–6 bullet points
first_paragraph_size:   18px (body-lg) — then 16px for remaining paragraphs

# ── CALLOUT BOX STRUCTURE ──────────────────────────
border:                 4px solid (left edge only), corner-radius 0 6px 6px 0
background:             #ffffff — always white, never tinted
label:                  0.6rem, weight 700, tracked caps, color matches border

# ── PULL QUOTE STRUCTURE ───────────────────────────
border:                 2px solid #1a1a24, top and bottom only
background:             none — transparent
attribution:            required, 0.72rem, ink-3
frequency:              maximum one per major section

# ── DEFINITION BOX STRUCTURE ───────────────────────
term:                   Space Grotesk 700, 1rem
rule:                   32px × 2px, accent color, below term
trigger:                first occurrence of each technical term only

# ── KEY STAT STRUCTURE ─────────────────────────────
number:                 Space Grotesk 800, minimum 2.5rem, accent color
label:                  Inter 400, 0.8rem, ink-2, max 3 lines
layout:                 1–3 columns, 1px border separators between cells
limit:                  maximum 3 stats per block
