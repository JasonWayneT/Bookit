Design Principles
1

Deep Dark Grounds

Near-black navy (2–8% lightness) anchors every scene. Darkness is the canvas, not the absence of design.

2

Selective Luminosity

Only key focal elements glow. Everything else stays dark. Restraint amplifies impact — one glowing point beats ten.

3

Flat Silhouette Forms

Characters are clean flat shapes. Posture, scale, and contrast carry all expression — no surface detail needed.

4

Geometric + Organic

Hard circuit geometry coexists with soft organic curves. The tension between the two creates visual energy.

Design Principles
1

Flat Color Blocks

Large areas of pure, saturated color with no internal gradients. The contrast between color fields does all structural work.

2

Rounded Everything

Generous radii on all shapes and characters. Soft geometry reads as approachable, playful, and universally accessible.

3

Conceptual Scale

Objects sized by narrative importance, not physical reality. A single idea can visually dwarf a galaxy.

4

Panel Rhythm

Diagonal splits, circles, and color-field panels create strong compositional rhythm without complex layout grids.

Design Principles
1

Dynamic Posing

Characters are always mid-action or mid-attitude. Static standing poses are reserved for UI roster screens only.

2

Elemental Identity

Each character owns a signature color doubling as their elemental or faction identity — consistent across UI and world.

3

Type as Composition

Display type acts as a visual element — overlapping characters, extreme scale, bold weight. Text and art are inseparable.

4

Cinematic Depth

Extreme foreground/background scale contrast creates cinematic depth without complex 3D environments.
